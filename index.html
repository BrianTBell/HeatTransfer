Heat Transfer

What is Heat Transfer?

Heat transfer is the study of energy movement due to temperature differences, critical in engineering for designing systems like engines, HVAC, and electronics. It encompasses three modes: conduction, where heat flows through solids; convection, involving heat transfer in fluids via motion; and radiation, where energy is emitted as electromagnetic waves. These principles govern thermal efficiency, cooling, and energy management in diverse applications.

1. Conduction

Fourier’s Law (1D): \( q = -k A \frac{dT}{dx} \)

Heat Flux: \( q'' = \frac{q}{A} = -k \frac{dT}{dx} \)

Thermal Resistance (Conduction): \( R_{\text{cond}} = \frac{L}{k A} \)

Steady-State Conduction (Plane Wall): \( q = \frac{k A (T_1 - T_2)}{L} \)

Cylindrical Conduction (Radial): \( q = \frac{2 \pi k L (T_1 - T_2)}{\ln(r_2 / r_1)} \)

Thermal Resistance (Cylindrical): \( R_{\text{cond}} = \frac{\ln(r_2 / r_1)}{2 \pi k L} \)

2. Convection

Newton’s Law of Cooling: \( q = h A (T_s - T_\infty) \)

Heat Transfer Coefficient: \( h = \frac{q}{A (T_s - T_\infty)} \)

Thermal Resistance (Convection): \( R_{\text{conv}} = \frac{1}{h A} \)

Nusselt Number: \( \text{Nu} = \frac{h L}{k} \)

Reynolds Number: \( \text{Re} = \frac{\rho v L}{\mu} \)

Prandtl Number: \( \text{Pr} = \frac{\nu}{\alpha} = \frac{c_p \mu}{k} \)

3. Radiation

Stefan-Boltzmann Law: \( q = \epsilon \sigma A T^4 \), \( \sigma = 5.67 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{W/(m}^2\text{·K}^4\text{)} \)

Net Radiation Exchange: \( q_{1-2} = \frac{\sigma A_1 (T_1^4 - T_2^4)}{1/\epsilon_1 + (1/\epsilon_2 - 1)(A_1 / A_2)} \)

View Factor Relation: \( \sum_{j=1}^n F_{i-j} = 1 \)

Radiation Resistance: \( R_{\text{rad}} = \frac{1 - \epsilon}{\epsilon A} + \frac{1}{A F_{1-2}} \)

4. Overall Heat Transfer

Overall Heat Transfer: \( Q = U A \Delta T_m \), \( U = \text{overall heat transfer coefficient} \)

Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD): \( \Delta T_m = \frac{\Delta T_1 - \Delta T_2}{\ln(\Delta T_1 / \Delta T_2)} \)

Overall Resistance: \( R_{\text{total}} = \sum R_i = \frac{1}{U A} \)

5. Transient Heat Transfer

Lumped Capacitance (Uniform Temperature): \( T(t) = T_\infty + (T_0 - T_\infty) e^{-\frac{h A}{\rho c V} t} \)

Biot Number: \( \text{Bi} = \frac{h L_c}{k} \), \( L_c = \frac{V}{A} \)

Fourier Number: \( \text{Fo} = \frac{\alpha t}{L_c^2} \), \( \alpha = \frac{k}{\rho c_p} \)

6. Heat Exchangers

Effectiveness-NTU Method: \( \epsilon = \frac{Q}{Q_{\text{max}}} \), \( Q_{\text{max}} = C_{\text{min}} (T_{h,\text{in}} - T_{c,\text{in}}) \)

Number of Transfer Units: \( \text{NTU} = \frac{U A}{C_{\text{min}}} \)

Capacity Ratio: \( C_r = \frac{C_{\text{min}}}{C_{\text{max}}} \)

7. Boiling and Condensation

Nucleate Boiling Heat Flux: \( q'' = \mu_l h_{fg} \left( \frac{g (\rho_l - \rho_v)}{\sigma} \right)^{1/2} \left( \frac{c_p (T_s - T_{\text{sat}})}{C_{s,f} h_{fg} \text{Pr}_l^n} \right)^3 \)

Film Condensation (Vertical Plate): \( h = 0.943 \left( \frac{g \rho_l (\rho_l - \rho_v) k_l^3 h_{fg}}{\mu_l L (T_{\text{sat}} - T_s)} \right)^{1/4} \)
